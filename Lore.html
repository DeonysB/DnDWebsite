Lore: The Heart of the Forgotten Realms

The Creation Myths

The story begins with chaos, where the first deities emerged to shape existence itself. Legends speak of Ao, the Overgod, who set the laws of the multiverse. From their hands came the prime worlds, life, and the very essence of magic. These myths remind us that creation itself was born from struggle and imagination.

The Planes of Existence

The universe is a tapestry of planes, each more wondrous and mysterious than the last. From the mortal-bound Material Plane to the vibrant Feywild and the shadowed decay of the Shadowfell, every realm offers a glimpse into the boundless creativity of the cosmos. The Astral Sea connects them all, a place of mystery and forgotten deities.

Gods and Pantheons

The gods of the Forgotten Realms are as varied as the worlds they preside over. Whether it’s Pelor, the god of light, or Tiamat, the fearsome dragon queen, each deity wields immense influence. Worshippers align with their chosen gods, seeking blessings, power, or purpose in a world teeming with divine intrigue.

The Great Wars

The history of the Realms is shaped by epic conflicts. The Dawn War saw gods battling primordials for creation’s control, while the Blood War rages eternally between devils and demons. Such wars ripple through time, leaving scars upon the land and tales of heroism in their wake.

Artifacts of Power

The Forgotten Realms are home to relics of unimaginable power. From the Hand and Eye of Vecna to the chaotic Deck of Many Things, each artifact holds the potential to change the fate of any who dare wield it. These treasures are both a blessing and a curse, often drawing adventurers into perilous quests.

The Adventurers' Call

Every adventurer hears the call—the whisper of untold riches, the allure of danger, or the chance to shape the world. Whether uncovering ancient ruins, defeating legendary foes, or forging bonds with unlikely allies, adventurers breathe life into the Realms. The world is a canvas for endless tales, and you are the artist.
